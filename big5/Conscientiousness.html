The Climate and Weather in Algiers and Marseille

Algiers and Marseille, both located along the Mediterranean coast, exhibit distinct climate characteristics influenced by their geographical settings. Here's a comparison of their weather and climate:

Algiers, Algeria, enjoys a Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. The average annual temperature is approximately 18.2°C, with summer highs reaching up to 30°C in August and winter lows dropping to around 6°C in January and February. Algiers receives about 537.7 mm of precipitation annually, with most rainfall occurring in the winter months. December is typically the wettest month, while July is the driest. The average humidity is around 75%, with higher levels in winter and lower levels in summer.

Marseille, France, also experiences a Mediterranean climate, with hot, dry summers and mild, rainy winters. The average annual temperature is slightly lower than Algiers at about 15.8°C. Summer temperatures typically range from 25°C to 28°C, while winter temperatures can drop to around 4°C in January. Marseille receives less rainfall than Algiers, averaging about 403.5 mm annually. November is often the rainiest month, while summer sees very little precipitation, making it ideal for outdoor activities. The humidity in Marseille is generally lower than in Algiers, contributing to its reputation as one of the sunniest cities in France, with an average humidity around 66%.
