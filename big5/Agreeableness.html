The Climate and Weather in Algiers and Marseille

Algiers and Marseille, both located along the Mediterranean coast, exhibit distinct climate characteristics influenced by their geographical settings. Here's a comparison of their weather and climate:

Algiers, Algeria

  • Temperature: Algiers experiences a Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. The average annual temperature is approximately 18.2°C. Summer highs can reach up to 30°C in August, while winter lows can drop to around 6°C in January and February.
  • Precipitation: Algiers receives about 537.7 mm of precipitation annually, with most rainfall occurring in the winter months. December is typically the wettest month, while July is the driest, receiving minimal rainfall.
  • Humidity: The average humidity is around 75%, with the highest levels in winter and the lowest during the summer months.

Marseille, France

  • Temperature: Marseille also has a Mediterranean climate, characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, rainy winters. The average annual temperature is slightly lower than Algiers at about 15.8°C. Summer temperatures typically range from 25°C to 28°C, while winter temperatures can drop to around 4°C (39.2°F) in January.
  • Precipitation: Marseille receives less rainfall than Algiers, averaging about 403.5 mm annually. November is often the rainiest month, while summer sees very little precipitation, making it ideal for outdoor activities.
  • Humidity: The humidity in Marseille is generally lower than in Algiers, contributing to its reputation as one of the sunniest cities in France. The average humidity ranges around 66%.
